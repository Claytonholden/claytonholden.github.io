# Topic 04 Notes — Final Reporting & Analyst Growth

## Writing a Professional Report

Structure I used:

1. Executive Summary
2. Network Overview
3. Baseline Traffic
4. Identified Anomalies
5. Evidence (IPs, ports, domains)
6. Assessment & Recommendations

No raw packet dumps.
No malware guessing.
Only observable behavior.

---

## Evidence-Based Conclusions

Strong report language:

Instead of:
“This is malware.”

Say:
“This behavior is consistent with automated command-and-control style traffic based on repeated outbound connections and structured byte patterns.”

---

## Analyst Perspective Shift

Beginning:
- Looked at packets individually.

End of course:
- Look at patterns first.
- Use statistics to guide deeper inspection.
- Justify conclusions with data.

---

## Career Application

These skills apply to:
- SOC Analyst
- Incident Response
- Threat Hunting
- Network Monitoring

Statistics-first thinking mirrors real SOC dashboards.
